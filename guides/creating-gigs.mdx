Gigs are the foundation of your freelance business on Khedma Market. This guide walks you through the complete 5-step process of creating a professional gig that attracts clients.

## Before you start

Before creating your gig, prepare:

- A clear service description
- Pricing strategy for your packages
- Portfolio images or work samples
- Common client questions and answers
- Keywords relevant to your service

<Tip>
Research similar gigs in your category to understand competitive pricing and what clients expect.
</Tip>

## Accessing the gig creation wizard

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to gigs page">
    From your dashboard, click on "Gigs" in the sidebar navigation.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Start creating">
    Click the "Add New" button in the top-right corner to open the gig creation wizard.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Step 1: Overview

The overview section establishes the foundation of your gig.

### Title

Your gig title must:
- Be at least 15 characters long
- Contain at least 4 words
- Be descriptive and clear
- Include relevant keywords

**Good examples:**
- "I will design a professional logo for your brand"
- "I will develop a responsive WordPress website"
- "I will write SEO-optimized blog posts for your business"

**Poor examples:**
- "Logo design" (too short)
- "I will design" (not specific enough)

<Tip>
Use natural language that includes keywords clients might search for. Think about what someone would type when looking for your service.
</Tip>

### Category and subcategory

1. **Select a category**: Choose the main category that best fits your service
2. **Select a subcategory**: Once you select a category, relevant subcategories will appear

The platform organizes services hierarchically:
- Category (e.g., "Design")
  - Subcategory groups (e.g., "Logo & Brand Identity")
    - Specific subcategories (e.g., "Logo Design")

<Note>
Choose the most specific subcategory that matches your service. This helps clients find you through category browsing.
</Note>

### Tags

Add up to 5 tags to help clients discover your gig:

- Use all 5 tag slots available
- Include relevant keywords and buzzwords
- Think about alternative search terms
- Consider related skills or technologies

**Example tags for a web design gig:**
- "responsive design"
- "UI/UX"
- "modern website"
- "landing page"
- "web designer"

<Tip>
Use all 5 tags to maximize your gig's discoverability in search results.
</Tip>

Once you've completed the overview section, click "Save & Continue" to proceed.

## Step 2: Pricing

Define your packages and pricing structure.

### Single package vs. multiple packages

You can offer:
- **Single package**: One Basic package with fixed pricing
- **Multiple packages**: Basic, Standard, and Premium tiers

Toggle "Offer Packages" to enable multiple packages.

<Tip>
Offering 3 packages gives clients more options and often leads to higher-value sales. Many clients choose the middle (Standard) option.
</Tip>

### Configuring each package

For each package, define:

#### Package name
A short, descriptive name (e.g., "Basic Logo", "Standard Website", "Premium Package")

#### Description
Describe what's included in this package:
- List specific deliverables
- Mention any limitations or extras
- Be clear and concise

**Example:**
```
1 custom logo design
3 revisions included
High-resolution files (PNG, JPG)
24-hour delivery
```

#### Delivery time

Select from preset options:
- 1-7 days (daily increments)
- 10, 14, or 21 days
- 30, 60, or 90 days

<Note>
Set realistic delivery times. Meeting deadlines is crucial for positive reviews and repeat business.
</Note>

#### Revisions

Choose the number of revisions included:
- 1-7 revisions
- Unlimited revisions

<Tip>
Consider offering more revisions in higher-tier packages to justify premium pricing.
</Tip>

#### Price

Enter your price in MAD (Moroccan Dirham):
- Consider your time and expertise
- Research competitor pricing
- Account for platform fees
- Price higher packages appropriately

**Pricing strategy example:**
- Basic: MAD 500 (starter option)
- Standard: MAD 1,200 (best value)
- Premium: MAD 2,500 (full service)

### Package differentiation

When offering multiple packages, differentiate them by:

- **Scope**: More deliverables in higher tiers
- **Quality**: Premium options with enhanced features
- **Speed**: Faster delivery for premium packages
- **Support**: More revisions or extended support
- **Extras**: Additional services bundled in

Click "Save & Continue" when your pricing is complete.

## Step 3: Description & FAQ

Provide detailed information about your service.

### Description

Use the rich text editor to create a comprehensive description:

**Include:**
- What you offer and how you work
- Your experience and qualifications
- What makes your service unique
- The process clients can expect
- Requirements from clients (if any)

**Structure suggestion:**
```
Introduction
- Brief overview of your service

What You'll Get
- Detailed list of deliverables
- File formats provided

My Process
1. Initial consultation
2. Design/development phase
3. Review and revisions
4. Final delivery

Why Choose Me
- Relevant experience
- Quality guarantees
- Communication commitment
```

<Tip>
Use formatting (headings, bullet points, bold text) to make your description easy to scan. Many clients skim rather than read every word.
</Tip>

### Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Add common questions clients ask:

1. Click "Add FAQ" to create a new question
2. Enter the question in the first field
3. Provide a clear answer in the text area
4. Click "Add" to save it

**Recommended FAQs:**
- "What do you need from me to get started?"
- "How many revisions are included?"
- "What file formats will I receive?"
- "Can you work with rush orders?"
- "What if I need changes after delivery?"

#### Managing FAQs

- Click on any FAQ to expand and edit it
- Update the question or answer as needed
- Click "Update" to save changes
- Click "Remove" to delete an FAQ

<Note>
A well-written FAQ section reduces back-and-forth messages and helps clients make confident purchasing decisions.
</Note>

Click "Save & Continue" to proceed to the gallery.

## Step 4: Gallery

Showcase your work with images and portfolio pieces.

### Uploading images

1. Click the upload area to select images
2. Choose high-quality images that represent your work
3. Upload multiple images to create a comprehensive gallery

### Image best practices

<Tip>
**Use high-quality images**: Upload clear, professional photos at high resolution.
</Tip>

<Tip>
**Show variety**: Include different styles or types of work to demonstrate your range.
</Tip>

<Tip>
**First image matters**: Your main image appears in search results, so make it count.
</Tip>

<Tip>
**Show process**: Consider including before/after images or work-in-progress shots.
</Tip>

### What to include

- **Completed projects**: Your best work samples
- **Process shots**: Show how you work
- **Variations**: Different styles or approaches
- **Results**: Demonstrate the impact of your work

<Note>
Only upload work you have permission to share. Don't use copyrighted images or client work without approval.
</Note>

Click "Save & Continue" after uploading your gallery.

## Step 5: Publish

Review and publish your gig.

### Review checklist

Before publishing, verify:

- [ ] Title is descriptive and includes keywords
- [ ] Category and subcategory are correct
- [ ] All 5 tags are used
- [ ] Package pricing makes sense
- [ ] Delivery times are realistic
- [ ] Description is comprehensive
- [ ] FAQ section addresses common questions
- [ ] Gallery includes high-quality images

### Publishing your gig

1. Review all sections one final time
2. Click "Publish" to make your gig live
3. Your gig will appear in search results immediately

<Tip>
Your gig is published immediately, but you can pause or edit it at any time from your Gigs page.
</Tip>

## After publishing

Once your gig is live:

### Monitor performance

- Track views and impressions in your dashboard
- Monitor how clients find your gig
- Note which keywords drive traffic

### Optimize over time

- Update your description based on client questions
- Add new portfolio images as you complete projects
- Adjust pricing based on demand
- Expand FAQ section with new common questions

### Promote your gig

- Share on social media
- Link from your website or blog
- Include in your email signature
- Mention to your existing client network

## Managing published gigs

From the Gigs page, you can:

- **Edit**: Update any section of your gig
- **Pause**: Temporarily hide your gig from search
- **Delete**: Permanently remove your gig
- **Duplicate**: Create a similar gig quickly

<Note>
Gigs in draft status won't appear in search results. Make sure to publish after creating or editing.
</Note>

## Common mistakes to avoid

- Using vague or generic titles
- Pricing too low to attract clients (this can signal low quality)
- Promising unrealistic delivery times
- Neglecting the description section
- Using poor-quality or irrelevant images
- Not utilizing all available tags
- Copying competitors' gig descriptions

## Tips for successful gigs

<Tip>
**Be specific**: Generic gigs get lost in search. Niche services stand out.
</Tip>

<Tip>
**Update regularly**: Refresh your gallery and description every few months.
</Tip>

<Tip>
**Respond quickly**: When clients message about your gig, reply within hours if possible.
</Tip>

<Tip>
**Deliver quality**: Your gig performance (reviews, completion rate) affects its search ranking.
</Tip>

Now you're ready to create compelling gigs that attract clients! Remember, your first gig is a starting point—you'll refine and improve it based on client feedback and market response.
