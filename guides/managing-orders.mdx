Effective order management is crucial for successful projects on Khedma Market. This guide covers everything from receiving orders to final delivery and reviews, for both freelancers and clients.

## Understanding orders

An order is created when:
- A client purchases one of your gigs (for freelancers)
- You purchase a gig from a freelancer (for clients)

Each order contains:
- Order details and requirements
- Selected package information
- Delivery deadline
- Payment amount
- Communication thread
- Order status

## Accessing your orders

From your dashboard:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to orders">
    Click "Orders" in the sidebar navigation.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="View orders table">
    See all your orders in a comprehensive table view.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Filter and sort">
    Use filters to find specific orders by status, date, or client/seller.
  </Step>
</Steps>

The Orders page displays all orders with scrollable horizontal viewing for complete information.

## Order information displayed

The orders table shows:

### For freelancers (selling)

- **Buyer**: Client's name and profile picture
- **Gig**: Which gig was purchased (thumbnail and title)
- **Status**: Current order status (Processing, In Progress, Completed, etc.)
- **Due on**: Delivery deadline date
- **Total**: Payment amount
- **Actions**: Quick actions menu

### For clients (buying)

- **Seller**: Freelancer's name and profile
- **Gig**: Service purchased (thumbnail and title)
- **Status**: Current order status
- **Due on**: Expected delivery date
- **Total**: Amount paid
- **Actions**: Available options

<Tip>
Click on any order row to view complete details and access the full order management interface.
</Tip>

## Order statuses

Orders move through several statuses:

### Processing

**Meaning**: Order just created, requirements being gathered

**Freelancer actions:**
- Review order details
- Request any needed information from client
- Confirm understanding of requirements

**Client actions:**
- Provide any additional requirements
- Share necessary files or assets
- Answer freelancer questions

### In Progress

**Meaning**: Freelancer actively working on the order

**Freelancer actions:**
- Work on deliverables
- Update client on progress
- Ask questions as needed

**Client actions:**
- Be available for questions
- Provide feedback if requested
- Monitor progress updates

### Delivered

**Meaning**: Freelancer submitted completed work

**Freelancer actions:**
- Wait for client review
- Be available for questions
- Prepare for potential revision requests

**Client actions:**
- Review delivered work
- Accept if satisfactory
- Request revisions if needed

### Completed

**Meaning**: Order finished, payment released

**Both parties:**
- Leave reviews
- Download final files
- Order history preserved

### Cancelled

**Meaning**: Order cancelled before completion

**Note**: Cancellations should be rare and mutually agreed upon.

## For freelancers: Managing incoming orders

### When you receive an order

<Steps>
  <Step title="Notification">
    You'll receive a notification about the new order.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Review requirements">
    Open the order to see what the client needs.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Request clarification">
    If anything is unclear, message the client immediately.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Start work">
    Begin working once you have all necessary information.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Clarifying requirements

If the client's requirements aren't clear:

1. Send a message through the order interface
2. Ask specific questions
3. Request missing files or information
4. Confirm your understanding before starting

<Tip>
**Ask early**: It's better to clarify requirements upfront than deliver wrong work. Clients appreciate thorough questions.
</Tip>

### Working on the order

During the work phase:

- **Track your time**: Ensure you can deliver by the deadline
- **Provide updates**: Let clients know progress, especially for longer projects
- **Maintain quality**: Deliver your best work every time
- **Document decisions**: Keep notes on choices made during the project

<Note>
Your delivery deadline is calculated from the order date plus the delivery time specified in your package. Plan accordingly.
</Note>

### Communicating with clients

**Best practices:**

- Respond to messages within 24 hours
- Be professional and friendly
- Set clear expectations
- Provide progress updates for multi-day orders
- Be proactive about potential delays

**If you need more time:**

1. Contact the client as soon as you realize
2. Explain the situation honestly
3. Propose a new deadline
4. Request deadline extension through the platform

<Tip>
Clients are usually understanding about delays if you communicate proactively. Silence until the deadline is what frustrates them.
</Tip>

### Delivering work

When your work is complete:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Prepare files">
    Organize all deliverables clearly and professionally.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Upload files">
    Use the delivery interface to upload completed work.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Add delivery message">
    Explain what you're delivering and how to use it.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Submit delivery">
    Mark the order as delivered to notify the client.
  </Step>
</Steps>

#### Delivery message template

```
Hi [Client Name],

I've completed your [project name] and delivered the following:

- [List each deliverable]
- [Include file formats]
- [Note any special instructions]

Please review the work and let me know if you need any revisions 
(you have [X] revisions included in your package).

Thank you for your order!

Best regards,
[Your Name]
```

### Handling revision requests

If the client requests revisions:

1. **Review the request**: Understand what needs to change
2. **Check package terms**: Confirm revisions are within package limits
3. **Respond promptly**: Acknowledge the request within 24 hours
4. **Complete revisions**: Make requested changes
5. **Redeliver**: Submit revised work through the order interface

<Note>
Revisions should be reasonable adjustments to the original scope. Major changes or scope creep may warrant a new order.
</Note>

### When scope expands

If the client requests work beyond the original package:

- Politely explain the additional work is outside the package scope
- Offer to create a custom order for the extra work
- Price the additional work fairly
- Don't feel obligated to provide free extras

<Tip>
Be firm but friendly about scope boundaries. Most clients respect reasonable limits when explained professionally.
</Tip>

## For clients: Managing orders you've placed

### After purchasing a gig

<Steps>
  <Step title="Provide requirements">
    Fill out the requirements form immediately after purchase.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Upload files">
    Share any necessary assets or resources.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Answer questions">
    Respond promptly to freelancer questions.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Monitor progress">
    Check order status and respond to updates.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Providing clear requirements

**Include:**
- Specific deliverables you expect
- Any brand guidelines or style preferences
- File format needs
- Important deadlines or milestones
- Examples of what you like/dislike
- Access to necessary accounts or systems

**Example requirements:**
```
Project: Logo design for coffee shop

Business name: The Daily Grind
Tagline: Fresh coffee, fresh perspective

Style preferences:
- Modern and minimalist
- Warm, inviting feeling
- Not too corporate

Colors: Earth tones preferred (browns, greens)

Files needed: AI, PNG, JPG in various sizes

Inspiration: [Link to Pinterest board]
```

<Tip>
The more specific your requirements, the more likely you'll get exactly what you want on the first delivery.
</Tip>

### Tracking order progress

- Check the Orders page regularly
- Review any progress updates from the freelancer
- Respond quickly to questions
- Be available as the deadline approaches

### Reviewing delivered work

When the freelancer delivers:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Download and review">
    Carefully examine all delivered files and work.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Check against requirements">
    Verify the delivery matches what you requested.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Test functionality">
    If applicable, test that everything works as expected.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Decide on action">
    Accept the delivery or request specific revisions.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Accepting delivery

If you're satisfied with the work:

1. Click "Accept" or "Approve" on the delivery
2. Leave a review for the freelancer
3. Payment is released to the freelancer
4. Download final files for your records

<Note>
Once you accept delivery, the order is complete and payment is released. Make sure you're satisfied before accepting.
</Note>

### Requesting revisions

If changes are needed:

1. Click "Request Revision"
2. Be specific about what needs to change
3. Explain why changes are needed
4. Provide examples if helpful
5. Be respectful and constructive

**Good revision request:**
```
The logo looks great, but could you make these adjustments:

1. Make the text slightly bolder for better readability
2. Change the background color to a warmer brown (#8B4513)
3. Increase spacing between the icon and text by about 10px

Everything else is perfect! Thank you.
```

**Poor revision request:**
```
I don't like it. Make it better.
```

<Tip>
Be specific and constructive with revision requests. Freelancers want to satisfy you but need clear direction.
</Tip>

### Revision limits

Remember:
- Each package includes a specific number of revisions
- Revisions should be reasonable adjustments to the original work
- Major changes or new directions may be outside revision scope
- Unlimited revision packages still expect reasonable requests

### If you're unhappy with the delivery

If the work doesn't meet expectations:

1. **Communicate clearly**: Explain specific issues
2. **Reference requirements**: Point to original agreement
3. **Request revisions**: Give the freelancer a chance to fix issues
4. **Contact support**: If resolution isn't reached, involve platform support

<Note>
Most issues are resolved through clear communication. Approach problems collaboratively rather than confrontationally.
</Note>

## Order communication

### Using order messages

All order-related communication should happen through the order interface:

- Keeps everything documented
- Protects both parties
- Allows support to help if needed
- Maintains professional boundaries

<Tip>
**Never communicate off-platform**: Keep all project discussion within Khedma Market for your protection.
</Tip>

### Communication best practices

**For everyone:**

- Be professional and respectful
- Respond within 24 hours
- Be clear and specific
- Use friendly but professional tone
- Provide context for questions
- Say thank you

**For freelancers:**

- Set expectations early
- Provide progress updates
- Ask questions promptly
- Explain technical decisions
- Be proactive about issues

**For clients:**

- Provide complete requirements upfront
- Answer questions quickly
- Give constructive feedback
- Be available near deadlines
- Acknowledge good work

## Completing orders

### Final steps

Once work is accepted:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Leave a review">
    Rate your experience and leave constructive feedback.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Download files">
    Save all deliverables to your system.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Close the order">
    Mark the order as complete in your records.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Writing reviews

**Good reviews include:**
- Overall experience rating
- What was done well
- Specific strengths (communication, quality, timeliness)
- Would you work together again?

**Example review (client about freelancer):**
```
Excellent work! [Freelancer name] delivered exactly what I needed, 
on time and with great communication throughout. The logo design 
exceeded my expectations. Highly recommended!
```

**Example review (freelancer about client):**
```
Great client to work with! Clear requirements, quick responses, 
and professional throughout. Would happily work with again.
```

<Tip>
Positive reviews help both freelancers and clients build reputation on the platform. Be generous with praise when deserved.
</Tip>

## Handling problems

### Common issues and solutions

**Missed deadline**
- Freelancer: Communicate early, request extension, offer compensation
- Client: Be understanding if communicated properly, work together on solution

**Unclear requirements**
- Ask clarifying questions immediately
- Don't proceed until requirements are clear
- Document all agreements

**Scope creep**
- Refer back to original requirements
- Discuss additional work as separate order
- Set clear boundaries professionally

**Poor communication**
- Set response time expectations
- Use clear, specific language
- Follow up if no response after 48 hours

**Quality issues**
- Provide specific, constructive feedback
- Use revision system appropriately
- Reference package terms and requirements

### When to contact support

Involve platform support if:
- Unable to reach the other party
- Serious quality or delivery issues
- Disputes about scope or payment
- Inappropriate behavior
- Technical problems with the platform

<Note>
Always try to resolve issues directly first. Support is available when direct resolution isn't possible.
</Note>
