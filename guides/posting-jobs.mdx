Posting jobs on Khedma Market allows you to receive applications from qualified freelancers and choose the best candidate for your needs. This guide covers everything from creating your job listing to reviewing applications.

## When to post a job

Consider posting a job instead of purchasing a gig when you need:

- **Multiple candidates to review**: Compare different freelancers before deciding
- **Long-term or full-time positions**: Hire for ongoing work relationships
- **Complex requirements**: Discuss details with candidates before committing
- **Team members**: Build your remote or hybrid team
- **Flexible arrangements**: Negotiate terms with individual freelancers

<Tip>
For one-off, well-defined tasks with fixed pricing, purchasing a gig is usually faster and more straightforward.
</Tip>

## Accessing the job posting form

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to jobs section">
    From your company dashboard, click "Jobs" in the sidebar navigation.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Create new job">
    Click the "Create a new job" button in the top-right corner.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Fill in the form">
    Complete all required fields in the job posting form.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Job posting fields

### Title

**Required field**

Create a clear, descriptive title for your position:

**Good examples:**
- "Senior Web App Designer"
- "Full-Stack JavaScript Developer"
- "Social Media Marketing Manager"
- "Content Writer - Technology Blog"

**Poor examples:**
- "Designer Needed" (too vague)
- "URGENT!!!! HIRE NOW" (unprofessional)
- "Web Person" (unclear role)

<Tip>
Use industry-standard job titles that freelancers search for. Avoid internal jargon or overly creative titles.
</Tip>

### Location

**Required field**

Specify where the work will be performed:

- City name for on-site or hybrid positions (e.g., "Agadir", "Casablanca")
- "Remote" if the position is fully remote
- "Morocco" for remote positions within the country

Example: `Agadir`

<Note>
The location field affects which freelancers see your job. Be accurate to attract relevant candidates.
</Note>

### Description

**Required field**

Provide comprehensive details about the position using the rich text editor.

#### Recommended structure

The platform provides a helpful template with three main sections:

##### 1. The Role

**What to include:**
- Clear outline of responsibilities and expectations
- Day-to-day tasks and deliverables
- How the role contributes to company goals
- Team structure and collaboration opportunities
- Tools and technologies used

**Example:**
```
We're seeking a Senior Web App Designer to lead the design of our 
customer-facing applications. You'll be responsible for:

- Creating user-centered designs for web applications
- Collaborating with engineers and product managers
- Conducting user research and usability testing
- Maintaining and evolving our design system
- Presenting design concepts to stakeholders

This role directly impacts our user experience and product success.
```

##### 2. About You

**What to include:**
- Required technical skills and experience
- Preferred qualifications
- Soft skills and work style
- Educational background (if relevant)
- Portfolio or work sample expectations

**Example:**
```
You should have:

- 5+ years of experience in web application design
- Strong portfolio demonstrating UX/UI expertise
- Proficiency in Figma, Sketch, or similar tools
- Understanding of front-end development (HTML/CSS)
- Excellent communication and presentation skills
- Genuine passion for creating exceptional user experiences
- Eagerness to learn and adapt to new design trends
```

##### 3. Things You Might Do

**What to include:**
- Variety of projects and tasks
- Growth opportunities
- Stakeholder interaction
- Special initiatives or challenges

**Example:**
```
In this role, you might:

- Design a new onboarding flow for our mobile app
- Conduct usability tests with actual users
- Collaborate with our engineering team on implementation
- Present design concepts to executive leadership
- Mentor junior designers on the team
- Contribute to our open-source design system
```

<Tip>
Use the rich text editor's formatting options (headings, lists, bold text) to make your job description easy to scan.
</Tip>

### Job Type

**Required field**

Select the employment type from the dropdown:

- **Full-time**: Standard 40-hour work week
- **Part-time**: Less than full-time hours
- **Contract**: Fixed-term or project-based
- **Freelance**: Independent contractor arrangement
- **Internship**: Learning or training position
- **Temporary**: Short-term position

Choose the option that best matches your needs.

<Note>
Be honest about the job type. Misrepresenting the nature of the position can lead to mismatched expectations.
</Note>

### Salary

**Optional field**

Provide salary information if you're comfortable sharing:

**Recommended formats:**
- "7,000 MAD - 9,000 MAD"
- "15,000 MAD per month"
- "Competitive salary based on experience"
- "DOE (Depends on Experience)"

**Benefits of including salary:**
- Attracts qualified candidates within your budget
- Reduces time spent on unaligned applications
- Shows transparency and respect for applicants' time

<Tip>
Including salary information typically results in more qualified applications and faster hiring.
</Tip>

### Can be remote

**Toggle switch**

Enable this option if:
- The position can be performed remotely
- You're open to remote work arrangements
- The role is remote-friendly or hybrid

This expands your candidate pool significantly.

<Note>
If you enable "Can be remote", make sure your job description clarifies:
- Whether it's fully remote or hybrid
- Any required office days
- Time zone requirements
- Remote work policies
</Note>

## Creating your job posting

<Steps>
  <Step title="Fill in all required fields">
    Complete the title, location, description, and job type fields.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Add optional information">
    Include salary details if appropriate and toggle remote options.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Preview your post">
    Review all information for accuracy and clarity.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Click Create">
    Submit your job posting to make it live immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Once created, your job appears in search results and freelancers can begin applying.

## After posting your job

### Managing job listings

From the Jobs page, you can:

- **View all jobs**: See your active, closed, and draft job postings
- **Edit jobs**: Update details at any time
- **Close jobs**: Stop accepting new applications
- **View applications**: Access all applications for each job

### Monitoring applications

You'll see application activity in your dashboard:

- **Daily applications chart**: Track application trends over the past 7 days
- **Applications by cities**: Geographic distribution of applicants
- **Total application count**: Number of applications per job

<Tip>
Check your dashboard regularly during the first few days after posting. Most applications arrive within 48 hours.
</Tip>

## Reviewing applications

### Accessing applications

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to jobs section">
    Navigate to the Jobs page from your dashboard.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Select a job">
    Click on any job listing to view its details.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="View applications">
    Access the applications tab to see all candidates who applied.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### What to review in each application

For each applicant, consider:

- **Profile information**: Experience, skills, and bio
- **Portfolio**: Past work samples and projects
- **Application message**: Their specific interest in your job
- **Location**: Where they're based
- **Availability**: When they can start
- **Rate or salary expectations**: Budget alignment

### Application review process

1. **Initial screening**: Review all applications to identify promising candidates
2. **Portfolio review**: Examine work samples in detail
3. **Shortlist**: Create a list of top candidates to interview
4. **Communication**: Reach out to shortlisted candidates via the platform
5. **Interview**: Conduct calls or video interviews
6. **Decision**: Choose your candidate and make an offer

<Tip>
Respond to all applications, even if just to say "thanks, but we're moving forward with other candidates." This builds goodwill in the community.
</Tip>

## Best practices for job postings

### Writing compelling job descriptions

<Tip>
**Be specific**: Detail exactly what you're looking for rather than using generic descriptions.
</Tip>

<Tip>
**Show personality**: Let your company culture come through in your writing.
</Tip>

<Tip>
**Focus on impact**: Explain how this role makes a difference, not just task lists.
</Tip>

<Tip>
**Set clear expectations**: Be upfront about requirements, responsibilities, and work arrangements.
</Tip>

### Attracting quality applications

- **Professional presentation**: Use proper grammar and formatting
- **Comprehensive details**: Provide enough information for freelancers to assess fit
- **Realistic requirements**: Don't ask for 10 years of experience in a 5-year-old technology
- **Competitive compensation**: Research market rates for your requirements
- **Quick responses**: Reply to applications within 48 hours

### Common mistakes to avoid

- **Vague descriptions**: "Looking for someone to do stuff"
- **Unrealistic expectations**: Senior-level work at junior-level pay
- **Copy-paste jobs**: Generic descriptions that don't reflect actual needs
- **Missing information**: No salary range, unclear about remote options
- **Slow responses**: Taking weeks to review applications

## Closing a job posting

When you've found your candidate:

1. Return to the Jobs page
2. Select the job you want to close
3. Click "Close Job" or similar action
4. The job will no longer accept new applications

<Note>
Close jobs promptly after hiring to respect applicants' time and keep your job listings current.
</Note>

## Reposting or editing jobs

### Editing an active job

- Click on any job from your Jobs list
- Click "Edit" to modify details
- Save changes to update the live posting

### Reposting a closed job

- Open the closed job listing
- Click "Repost" to make it active again
- Update details if needed before reposting

## Tips for successful hiring

<Tip>
**Cast a wide net initially**: Consider more applicants than you think you need.
</Tip>

<Tip>
**Look beyond technical skills**: Communication, reliability, and cultural fit matter.
</Tip>

<Tip>
**Ask for specific examples**: Request work samples relevant to your project.
</Tip>

<Tip>
**Start with a trial**: Consider a small paid project before committing to long-term work.
</Tip>

<Tip>
**Be transparent**: Clear communication about expectations prevents problems later.
</Tip>

## Managing multiple job postings

If you're hiring for multiple positions:

- Create separate postings for each distinct role
- Use clear titles to differentiate positions
- Track applications separately for each job
- Close jobs individually as positions fill
